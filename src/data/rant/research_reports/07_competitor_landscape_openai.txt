# Competitor Landscape

**Twist Bioscience (founded 2013)** – A public company (raised ~$200 M pre-IPO ([www.twistbioscience.com](https://www.twistbioscience.com/blog/company-news-updates/year-review-twist-bioscience-expands-2017#:~:text=8))) with a semiconductor-based oligo synthesis platform.  Twist generates thousands of short oligos per silicon chip and assembles genes from them.  It sells **gene fragments** from $0.07/nt and NGS-verified **clonal genes** from $0.09/nt (e.g. a 1 kb clone ≈$90, 10-day lead time) ([www.twistbioscience.com](https://www.twistbioscience.com/products/genes/gene-synthesis?tab=oligo-pools#:~:text=Twist%20Bioscience%E2%80%99s%20high%20precision%2C%20silicon,for%20the%20price%20you%20want)) ([www.twistbioscience.com](https://www.twistbioscience.com/products/genes/gene-synthesis?tab=oligo-pools#:~:text=0)).  Turnaround is 2–4 business days for fragments and 4–10 days for clonal genes ([www.twistbioscience.com](https://www.twistbioscience.com/products/genes/gene-synthesis?tab=oligo-pools#:~:text=0)).  Throughput is very high (e.g. ~9,600 genes per chip ([golden.com](https://golden.com/wiki/Twist_Bioscience-PBWG9NW#:~:text=Twist%20Bioscience%20can%20achieve%201%2C000x,using%20traditional%20DNA%20synthesis%20technologies)) and on the order of 150–200 k genes/year ([www.sec.gov](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1581280/000162828024003051/twst-20231231.htm#:~:text=match%20at%20L1666%20Number%20of,14))).  However, producing clonal, sequence-verified DNA remains *labor intensive* and costly compared to raw pools ([www.twistbioscience.com](https://www.twistbioscience.com/products/genes/gene-synthesis?tab=oligo-pools#:~:text=Twist%20Bioscience%E2%80%99s%20high%20precision%2C%20silicon,for%20the%20price%20you%20want)).  Twist has aggressively pushed oligo-pool and library products (e.g. selling 1 Gb to Ginkgo) ([www.twistbioscience.com](https://www.twistbioscience.com/blog/company-news-updates/year-review-twist-bioscience-expands-2017#:~:text=1,DNA%20Go%20to%20Ginkgo%20Bioworks)), but may see clonal assembly as its value-added product.  (There is community concern that Twist could limit pool sales to gene-assemblers, since it also offers its own gene synthesis service.)  Recent SEC filings show Twist shipped ~171,000 genes in 2023 (up from 134,000 in 2022) ([www.sec.gov](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1581280/000162828024003051/twst-20231231.htm#:~:text=match%20at%20L1666%20Number%20of,14)), with synthetic-gene revenue ~$20 M (30% of Q4 2023 sales) ([www.sec.gov](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1581280/000162828024003051/twst-20231231.htm#:~:text=Synthetic%20genes%20%20,)).  Note: despite high volume, Twist still loses money (net loss $43M in Q4’23) ([www.sec.gov](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1581280/000162828024003051/twst-20231231.htm#:~:text=match%20at%20L1532%20We%20have,Since%20our%20inception%2C%20we%20have)).

**Ansa Biotechnologies (ca. 2020)** – A VC-backed startup (Series A: $68 M in 2022 ([www.biocentury.com](https://www.biocentury.com/article/643096/ansa-debuts-with-68m-series-a-for-enzymatic-dna-synthesis-hold-the-blocking-groups#:~:text=Ansa%20aims%20to%20stand%20out,to%20launch%20its%20service%20offerings)); Series B: $54 M in 2025) developing **enzymatic DNA synthesis**.  Ansa’s platform uses polymerases with chemically-cleavable linkers instead of phosphoramidite chemistry ([www.biocentury.com](https://www.biocentury.com/article/643096/ansa-debuts-with-68m-series-a-for-enzymatic-dna-synthesis-hold-the-blocking-groups#:~:text=Ansa%20aims%20to%20stand%20out,to%20launch%20its%20service%20offerings)).  It promises direct synthesis of “up to 600 bp” without assembly (sequences that other vendors find hard to build) and *assembly-free* constructs up to 50 kb ([ansabio.com](https://ansabio.com/#:~:text=scientific%20projects)).  It aims to undercut oligo-pool/assembly workflows with faster service.  Products include custom gene synthesis (clonal DNA up to multi-kb length) and plasmids (they recently announced an “ultra-long clonal DNA service” up to clinical scale ([ansabio.com](https://ansabio.com/news-events/news/ansa-biotechnologies-targets-nucleic-acid-synthesis-market-with-ultra-long-clonal-dna-service/#:~:text=Ansa%20Biotechnologies%20Targets%20Nucleic%20Acid,win%20a%20slice%20of%20the))).  Pricing is not public, but Ansa claims “industry’s best price” and guaranteed on-time delivery.  No firm turnaround is announced, but likely on the order of 1–2 weeks once launched.  Ansa is still private and investing heavily (burn rate unknown); it has said it will sell DNA as a service rather than machines.  **Competition**: Ansa’s enzymatic approach targets many of the same applications as oligo pools + assembly, promising to leapfrog traditional synthesis.  If successfully commercialized, it could undercut pool suppliers by making longer constructs directly.

**Elegen (San Francisco, founded 2017)** – A private company (Series B $35 M in 2024 ([www.synbiobeta.com](https://www.synbiobeta.com/read/elegen-reels-in-35m-to-turbocharge-dna-synthesis#:~:text=Image))) pursuing **cell-free, microfluidic gene synthesis**.  Its ENFINIA™ platform combines droplet microfluidics, AI, and a “split-pool” synthesizer with a proprietary cell-free (non-plasmid) cloning method ([elegenbio.com](https://elegenbio.com/technology/#:~:text=The%20GEN%20II%20platform%20incorporates,diversity%20gene%20synthesis)) ([www.businesswire.com](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20250305179436/en/Elegen-Cuts-Pricing-for-ENFINIA-DNA-Expanding-Access-to-Next-Generation-Cell-Free-DNA-Manufacturing#:~:text=ENFINIA%20DNA%20is%20uniquely%20manufactured,immediate%20use%20in%20customer%20workflows)). Elegen delivers very long, NGS-verified sequences: linear DNA up to ~7 kb and clonal plasmid genes up to ~15 kb ([www.businesswire.com](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20250305179436/en/Elegen-Cuts-Pricing-for-ENFINIA-DNA-Expanding-Access-to-Next-Generation-Cell-Free-DNA-Manufacturing#:~:text=ENFINIA%20DNA%20is%20uniquely%20manufactured,immediate%20use%20in%20customer%20workflows)) ([www.businesswire.com](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20250305179436/en/Elegen-Cuts-Pricing-for-ENFINIA-DNA-Expanding-Access-to-Next-Generation-Cell-Free-DNA-Manufacturing#:~:text=Earlier%20this%20year%20the%20company,few%20as%2010%20business%20days)).  It recently reduced prices by 25%, now as low as **$0.15 per bp** for ENFINIA DNA (e.g. a 5 kb gene for ~$750) ([www.businesswire.com](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20250305179436/en/Elegen-Cuts-Pricing-for-ENFINIA-DNA-Expanding-Access-to-Next-Generation-Cell-Free-DNA-Manufacturing#:~:text=learning%20have%20increased%20synthesis%20success,further%20discounts%20for%20bulk%20orders)).  Turnaround is ~6–8 days for linear DNA and ~10 days for plasmids ([www.businesswire.com](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20250305179436/en/Elegen-Cuts-Pricing-for-ENFINIA-DNA-Expanding-Access-to-Next-Generation-Cell-Free-DNA-Manufacturing#:~:text=learning%20have%20increased%20synthesis%20success,further%20discounts%20for%20bulk%20orders)) ([www.businesswire.com](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20250305179436/en/Elegen-Cuts-Pricing-for-ENFINIA-DNA-Expanding-Access-to-Next-Generation-Cell-Free-DNA-Manufacturing#:~:text=Earlier%20this%20year%20the%20company,few%20as%2010%20business%20days)).  Throughput: It can run many constructs in parallel via its automated platform, including an early-access Gen II synthesizer launched in 2025.  Elegen emphasizes high automation and low failure rate (claimed “zero build failures” ([www.builtdna.com](https://www.builtdna.com/news/built-biotechnologies-expands-dna-manufacturing-leadership-and-onboards-50-new-customers#:~:text=%E2%80%9CResearchers%20advancing%20ambitious%20genome%20engineering,%E2%80%9D))).  It targets biotech/pharma R&D and CGT (mRNA/DNA templates) rather than academic labs.  **Current status:** Elegen is scaling up (added GMP products for clinical use) and, although not acquired by Danaher, it collaborates with large partners (e.g. GSK). It continues raising funds and expanding capacity.

**BUILT Biotechnologies (“BuiltDNA,” Charlottesville, est. ~2019)** – A small startup (Commonwealth BioAccelerator cohort; founder George McArthur) offering **DNA assembly-as-a-service**.  BuiltDNA uses a proprietary “Regenerative DNA Assembly™” approach: it recycles validated DNA fragments in a growing parts inventory to build new constructs ([www.builtdna.com](https://www.builtdna.com/#:~:text=Powered%20by%20our%20Regenerative%20DNA,for%20mRNA%20and%20saRNA%20production)).  **Products:** sequence-perfect clonal plasmids (in a custom “Standard BUILT Backbone”), fully synthetic whole-plasmids (“WPS”), linear DNA (for IVT templates), and custom plasmid libraries ([www.builtdna.com](https://www.builtdna.com/#:~:text=Powered%20by%20our%20Regenerative%20DNA,rich%20collections%20for%20high)).  The service is in early-access but expanding (50+ customers by late 2025 ([www.builtdna.com](https://www.builtdna.com/news/built-biotechnologies-expands-dna-manufacturing-leadership-and-onboards-50-new-customers#:~:text=BUILT%20Biotechnologies%2C%20Inc,for%20genome%20engineering%20and%20beyond))).  Pricing and exact turnaround are not published; they market it as “fast and cost-effective” ([www.builtdna.com](https://www.builtdna.com/#:~:text=BUILT%20DNA%E2%84%A2)) and claim unmatched speed/reliability ([www.builtdna.com](https://www.builtdna.com/news/built-biotechnologies-expands-dna-manufacturing-leadership-and-onboards-50-new-customers#:~:text=%E2%80%9CResearchers%20advancing%20ambitious%20genome%20engineering,%E2%80%9D)).  Throughput is gaining: they relocated to a larger facility and added staff in 2025 ([www.builtdna.com](https://www.builtdna.com/news/built-biotechnologies-expands-dna-manufacturing-leadership-and-onboards-50-new-customers#:~:text=years%2C%20I%20know%20how%20critical,%E2%80%9D)).  **Comparison to Stage 2:** BUILT’s model overlaps our Stage 2: it offers modular Golden-Gate assembly of parts into plasmids. Unlike Genetic Assemblies, BUILT relies on its own vectors and parts database (not open-source libraries). It focuses on larger-scale or “difficult” builds. We expect BuiltDNA is venture-backed (or grant-supported) but funding details are not public.

**Clutch Bio (Burlingame, CA, 2023)** – An early-stage company providing **plasmid design software plus Golden-Gate cloning service** ([clutchbio.com](https://clutchbio.com/#:~:text=Cloning%2C%20simplified)).  Clutch offers a SaaS tool (“Genie”) for streamlining plasmid design and assembly using custom “DNA toolkits”, and then physically builds the constructs.  They use modular DNA toolkits and Golden-Gate cloning to speed builds ([clutchbio.com](https://clutchbio.com/#:~:text=We%20employ%20modular%20DNA%20toolkits,achieve%20fast%20plasmid%20build%20times)). Turnaround is claimed “less than 1 week” for designs using existing parts ([clutchbio.com](https://clutchbio.com/#:~:text=software%20platform%2C%20biorepository%20service%2C%20and,of%20less%20than%201%20week)).  Deliverables include sequence-verified purified plasmids and saving parts in a biorepository ([clutchbio.com](https://clutchbio.com/#:~:text=)).  No public pricing; presumably fees are custom quoted per project.  Clutch targets R&D labs that need single or small batches of plasmids and want to offload cloning work.  **Comparison:** Clutch’s service model is similar to our vision of a “cloning foundry,” but at a smaller scale (one-by-one plasmids) and with human-managed workflows.  Genetic Assemblies differs by using full automation on chips (higher throughput) and aiming at synthesis from oligo pools rather than just recombining existing parts.

**Plasmidsaurus (Eugene, OR, ~2017)** – A commercial **whole-plasmid sequencing** service using Oxford Nanopore long reads ([plasmidsaurus.com](https://plasmidsaurus.com/plasmid_sequencing#:~:text=We%20pioneered%20whole%20plasmid%20sequencing,the%20headaches%20and%20get%20answers)).  Rather than synthesize DNA, they sequence it. Plasmidsaurus processes colonies or preps overnight, returning full plasmid sequences (including insert and backbone) often within 12–24 hours ([plasmidsaurus.com](https://plasmidsaurus.com/plasmid_sequencing#:~:text=Overnight%20results%2C%20zero%20extra%20steps)) ([plasmidsaurus.com](https://plasmidsaurus.com/plasmid_sequencing#:~:text=Accelerate%20your%20research%20with%20Plasmidsaurus,you%20do%20with%20more%20time)). Pricing per plasmid is low (on the order of tens of dollars per plasmid); they advertise “over 70,000 scientists” and can multiplex samples.  Throughput is high (millions of plasmids sequenced so far).  They do not currently offer DNA synthesis or assembly, but are a key partner: many gene synthesis customers use them for rapid QC.  They’re expanding into related services (e.g. AAV genome sequencing in 2025).  **Position:** Not a competitor in synthesis, but an adjacent service. They could integrate with synthesis providers (e.g. offering QC sequencing) or be acquired by one. Plasmidsaurus raised seed funding (Y-Combinator alum), but little beyond that.

**Other players:**  
- **Stilla Technologies (France)** – Mainly sells droplet/digital PCR systems (Juno, Naica) for genomics. Not a DNA synth company; more relevant to diagnostics.  
- **DNA Script (France/US)** – Developed a bench-top **enzymatic DNA synthesizer (“SYNTAX™”)** for short oligos (up to ~80 nt) ([ansabio.com](https://ansabio.com/#:~:text=scientific%20projects)). Funded by many VCs (~$95 M raised by 2022). They target oligo synthesis on-site. They do *not* currently assemble large synthetic genes.  
- **Molecular Assemblies** – Enzymatic DNA synthesis startup (spun out of Cambridge) aiming for long oligonucleotides and genes via proprietary reversible terminator chemistry. It has raised a few million and is working on instrument development.  
- **Codex DNA (Bio-Rad)** – Formerly Gen9, acquired by Eurofins and now part of Bio-Rad/IDT. They sold the **BioXp™** bench-top gene synthesizer (cloning and assembly unit) and still offer gene synthesis services. The BioXp automates PCR-based synthesis up to ~3 kb and uses Gibson assembly internally. Pricing was roughly comparable to other gene services when active (e.g. ~$0.10–0.25/bp depending on length). Current status: Bio-Rad still markets BioXp machines and offers service, but this is a niche compared to Twist.

## Positioning of Genetic Assemblies

Genetic Assemblies’ **“oligo-pool-to-clone” cloud lab** fits neatly between large foundry providers (Twist, Elegen) and small-scale services (Clutch, individual synth labs).  Unlike traditional gene synthesis, GA’s platform is designed for *low-labor, rapid assembly* of user-defined genes from pool-synthesized oligos.  We effectively offer on-demand, sequence-verified plasmids at roughly **$30 per gene (1–1.2 kb)** with ~3-day turnaround (comparable to Twist’s clonal prices ([www.twistbioscience.com](https://www.twistbioscience.com/products/genes/gene-synthesis?tab=oligo-pools#:~:text=Twist%20Bioscience%E2%80%99s%20high%20precision%2C%20silicon,for%20the%20price%20you%20want)) but faster/TAT than typical 10–14 day).  Our full automation (liquid handlers, sequencers, cloud LIMS) enables throughput ~700 clones/week per automated station, driving down labor cost. 

Our unique niche is exploiting **open-source parts and recursive Golden-Gate assembly** (Stage 2).  We intend to host a growing parts library so customers can snap together new constructs quickly (competing with Custom plasmid assembly).  Combined with Stage 3 protein expression, our value-add is a seamless design‐build‐test pipeline for biologists. In short, GA positions itself as a high-throughput “DNA foundry” – offering faster, cheaper clonal genes than Twists’s labor-heavy clones ([www.twistbioscience.com](https://www.twistbioscience.com/products/genes/gene-synthesis?tab=oligo-pools#:~:text=Twist%20Bioscience%E2%80%99s%20high%20precision%2C%20silicon,for%20the%20price%20you%20want)), while also providing modular assembly tools (like BUILT DNA or Clutch) but fully robotic. Our competitive edge is automating the full workflow (oligos→assemblies→verified clones→expressed protein) at scale, enabling a “cloud DNA lab” service that no one else currently provides.
