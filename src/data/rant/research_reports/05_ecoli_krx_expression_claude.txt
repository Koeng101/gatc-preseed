# Market Research Report: KRX-Based Automated Protein Expression for Genetic Assemblies

## 1. E. coli KRX Strain Details

### Manufacturer & Availability


E. coli KRX is a commercially available derivative of E. coli K-12, created by Promega (catalog no. L3002, Madison, WI, USA).

 

It combines characteristics that make it a suitable host for efficient plasmid transformation and propagation, as well as high-yield protein production with tight regulation.



### The Rhamnose-Inducible T7 System — How It Works


KRX cells contain a chromosomal copy of the T7 RNA polymerase driven by a rhamnose promoter (rhaBAD) that provides tight control of the proteins expressed via a T7 promoter.

 

Addition of rhamnose induces the expression of the T7 RNA polymerase, which in turn transcribes the gene of interest under control of a T7 promoter.



The regulation has multiple layers of control: 

In the presence of preferred carbon sources like glucose, cAMP concentrations are low and CRP does not activate transcription. Upon depletion of glucose, cAMP levels rise and CRP can activate transcription at rhaPBAD. L-rhamnose can bind to RhaR, which results in production of active RhaS. RhaS also binds rhamnose, binding the rhaPBAD promoter, resulting in production of high levels of T7 RNA polymerase.



Crucially, 

in E. coli KRX, the genes involved in the rhamnose metabolism have been deleted, ensuring a long induction phase.

 This means rhamnose is not consumed as a carbon source and continues to act as an inducer.

### Advantages Over BL21(DE3) for Cloning Compatibility

**Tight regulation (key for toxic proteins and single-step cloning/expression):**


When the KRX strain was used to express firefly luciferase protein, the precise control of the rhamnose operon resulted in a dramatic induction ratio of 1,700-fold upon addition of rhamnose, whereas the leaky IPTG-inducible T7 RNA polymerase-based system in BL21(DE3)-derived strains only showed an 8- to 43-fold induction ratio, primarily due to high pre-induction levels of protein expression.



**Dual-use strain (clone + express in the same cells):**


KRX enables high transformation efficiencies via the popular recA1, endA1, hsdr17, and e14− gene alleles, preventing the degradation of plasmid DNA and unwanted recombination events.

 

KRX is an E. coli K12 derivative that has salient features associated with cloning and screening strains plus engineered attributes to optimize controlled protein expression. Attributes that make this a good cloning strain are the partially defective restriction systems (hsd and e14), a lack of the most common nuclease that copurifies with plasmid DNA (endA–), and a preventive mutation to minimize undesirable recombination events (recA–).



**This is the critical strategic advantage for Genetic Assemblies:** Because KRX is both a cloning strain AND an expression strain, you can transform, clone, sequence, and express all in the same cells — eliminating the need to re-transform into a separate expression strain like BL21(DE3).

### Published Expression Yields: KRX vs. BL21(DE3)


Protein expression levels in KRX for three additional proteins were shown to be as high, or higher than, levels in BL21(DE3)-derived strains.

 

After induction, KRX exhibited the highest yield of luciferase, while BL21(DE3)pLysS had such poor yield that the protein band was barely visible.

 

This indicates that most of the luciferase expressed in BL21(DE3) is nonfunctional.





KRX is widely used for protein expression and protein evolution experiments, e.g., for cytochrome P450, peptide synthases, laccases, and Argonaute proteins, in certain cases showing markedly higher yields than other E. coli strains.
