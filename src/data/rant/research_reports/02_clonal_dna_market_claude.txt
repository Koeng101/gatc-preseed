| 2024–2025 | **$0.09/bp** | Unchanged for years |

A typical 1,000 bp gene at Twist has been **~$90–$125 all-in** (including cloning) for essentially the entire history of the product. Compare this to oligos, which dropped from ~$0.50/base to ~$0.05/base over 15 years.

### Why Clonal Gene Prices Haven't Dropped Like Oligos:

1. **Oligo synthesis scales on chemistry; clonal genes require biology.** Oligo synthesis is purely chemical (phosphoramidite coupling on solid phase or silicon), lending itself to semiconductor-style cost curves. Clonal genes require living cells (E. coli transformation), which introduces biological variability, failure modes, and labor-intensive troubleshooting that don't scale the same way.

2. **Labor is the bottleneck, not chemistry.** As detailed in Section 3, the downstream workflow (transformation, picking, miniprep, sequencing) accounts for ~50–60% of COGS. This labor hasn't been fully automated at most providers. Even Twist, despite their advanced front-end, still uses conventional cloning workflows.

3. **Twist's strategic pricing.** Twist is still unprofitable and working toward EBITDA breakeven. 

Since inception, Twist has incurred an accumulated net deficit of $1,319.6 million.

 

Twist expects to maintain gross margin above 50% and achieve adjusted EBITDA breakeven by end of fiscal 2026.

 Dropping prices would delay this goal. They are rationally choosing margin recovery over market expansion through price cuts.

4. **Oligopoly pricing dynamics.** 

The top three players — GenScript, Thermo Scientific, and IDT — collectively hold over 53% of the global gene synthesis market share.

 With Twist as the fourth major player, this concentrated market allows price discipline. GenScript's FLASH Gene at $89 flat rate actually *increased* the average price for short genes (a 500 bp gene at $0.09/bp = $45 at Twist, vs. $89 at GenScript FLASH).

5. **Express premiums replace price reductions.** Rather than lowering base prices, Twist has added premium Express services with "dynamic pricing." 

Twist's Express Genes are offered with dynamic pricing, which reflects market demand and manufacturing capacity in a responsive premium price.

 This actually *raises* average revenue per gene.

6. **Elegen is the first real disruptor on price.** 

Elegen's GEN II Cell-Free DNA Platform offers clonal, cell-free DNA at the price of non-clonal fragments. This capability unlocks longer construct lengths and broader design flexibility.

 Their $0.07/bp for linear and $0.12/bp for plasmid represents the first meaningful price disruption — and notably, Elegen achieves this by **eliminating the biological cloning step entirely** via cell-free technology.

> **Strategic Insight for Genetic Assemblies:** The reason prices haven't fallen is structural — the cloning workflow requires labor. If you automate that workflow end-to-end, you can profitably undercut the market at $30/gene because your COGS structure fundamentally differs from competitors who still rely on manual bench work for the 50–60% of costs that come from cloning.
