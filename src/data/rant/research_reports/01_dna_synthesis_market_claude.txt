## 5. Key Technology Transitions & Impact

### Column-Based → Chip-Based → Enzymatic Synthesis

| Generation | Technology | Era | Oligo Length | Error Rate | Cost Impact |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1st Gen** | Column-based phosphoramidite | 1980s–present | ~100–150 nt | ~1:200 (0.5%) | $0.05–$0.17/base |
| **2nd Gen** | Microarray / chip-based | 2010s–present | ~150–300 nt | ~1:300–1:500 | 10–100× cheaper oligo pools |
| **3rd Gen** | Enzymatic (TdT-based) | 2020s–emerging | ~200–1,000 nt | improving | Potentially cheaper, greener |

**Column-based (phosphoramidite chemistry):**
- 

Column-based synthesizers typically produce oligonucleotides at scales of 10 to 1,000 nmol with costs ranging between $0.05 and $0.17 per base, synthesizable up to ~100 nt with error rates at or below 0.5%.


- 

Column-based coupling yields are generally quite efficient (typically >99% per coupling), but the yield of full-length oligonucleotides decreases with increasing length.



**Chip-based / microarray (Twist, Agilent, GenScript GenTitan):**
- 

Twist's silicon platform achieves costs of $0.003/base for 300 bp fragments, and can produce 9,600 genes on a single silicon chip, representing a substantial leap in throughput.


- 

However, array-synthesized oligonucleotides are generally lower quality than column-synthesized counterparts, manifesting as sequence errors in assembled DNA, requiring some form of error reduction strategy.


- 

In May 2023, GenScript unveiled GenTitan, the first-ever miniature commercial semiconductor platform that uses integrated circuits for DNA synthesis with high throughput.



**Enzymatic synthesis (emerging):**
- 

For decades, DNA has been made through phosphoramidite chemistry—a process that uses harsh reagents, generates chemical waste, and hits a wall at around 200 base pairs. Anything longer requires assembly, which introduces errors.


- 

Ansa's enzymatic DNA synthesis technology is based on TdT-dNTP conjugates, enabling rapid and controlled single-base additions. The company has demonstrated direct synthesis of DNA molecules more than 1,000 bases long.


- 

In January 2025, DNA Script launched an advanced enzymatic DNA synthesis platform in France that produces custom oligonucleotides up to 500 nucleotides long, overcoming traditional chemical synthesis limits.
